Pro Se Legal Desk gives self-represented litigants a private procedural workspace for complaint preparation, filing steps, court access, deadlines, rule-linked calendar events, and procedural checklists.

Complaint Format Framework

  • Caption
  • Parties
  • Jurisdiction
  • Venue
  • Standing, where relevant
  • Factual allegations in numbered paragraphs
  • Causes of action or claims for relief
  • Request for relief
  • Jury demand, if applicable
  • Signature block
  • Certificate of service, if required at that stage

Pleading Standard Education

Federal pleading education should explain that a complaint generally needs enough factual content to make the claim plausible rather than merely possible. Users should read the applicable rules and cases and may use AI as a drafting companion, but the platform should not decide what facts or claims belong in their complaint.

Pro Se Practical Tips

  • Use numbered paragraphs.
  • Keep each paragraph focused on one event, fact, or procedural point.
  • Separate the background facts from the legal claims.
  • Do not rely only on conclusions.
  • Make the court’s jurisdiction clear.
  • Make clear who each party is.
  • Only represent yourself unless you are legally authorized to represent another person or entity.
  • Use AI as a companion for organization, plain-language explanation, and proofreading, not as a substitute for verifying rules and deadlines.

Suggested AI Companion Use

Users may use AI to help organize, outline, summarize public rules, improve readability, or check for missing procedural sections. Users remain responsible for verifying all law, rules, deadlines, claims, facts, and filings.

Common Filing Items

  • Original complaint
  • Copies required by the court
  • Summons for each defendant
  • Civil cover sheet
  • Filing fee or fee-waiver application
  • Certificate of interested persons, if required by local rules
  • Mailing envelope or in-person filing plan, if filing on paper

N.D. Texas Example

For paper filing, N.D. Texas local materials state that a plaintiff must provide the clerk an original complaint, a copy of the complaint and completed summons form for each defendant to be served, a civil cover sheet, filing fee or IFP application, and a separately signed certificate of interested persons.

Filing Method

  • In person at the clerk’s office.
  • By mail, if the court allows it.
  • Electronically, if the court permits pro se electronic filing.

Steps After Filing

  • Save the filed complaint.
  • Save the case number.
  • Confirm summons issuance.
  • Review docket entries.
  • Set up PACER access for federal court records.
  • Review whether pro se electronic filing is available in that court.
  • Calendar service and response deadlines.

Federal Response Deadline Education

Under FRCP 12(a), a defendant generally must serve an answer within 21 days after being served with the summons and complaint unless another time applies.

FRCP 12: Defenses and Objections / Time to Respond

Checklist

  • Enter service date.
  • Enter response deadline.
  • Check docket regularly.
  • Save any answer, motion, or extension request.
  • Calendar any response deadline if a motion is filed.

FAQ

Does this provide legal advice?

No. It provides procedural education, rule links, calendars, checklists, and organizational tools.

Do users enter case facts?

No. The workspace is built around court location, procedural stage, user-entered dates, and general procedural notes.

What is the paid feature?

Gated procedural pages, complaint preparation education, filing resources, PACER/court access guidance, calendar, rule links, and story submissions.

Pro Se Legal Desk provides educational resources and organizational tools. It does not provide legal advice, create an attorney-client relationship, recommend legal strategy, predict case outcomes, calculate deadlines automatically, or represent users in court. Users are responsible for verifying all deadlines against court orders, applicable rules, local requirements, and current law.
