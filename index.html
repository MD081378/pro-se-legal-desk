Start Here

Before drafting or filing paperwork, begin with the procedure. Court cases often turn on deadlines, filing requirements, service rules, local rules, and court orders.

1. Identify the Court

Figure out whether your matter belongs in federal court, state court, county court, municipal court, or another forum. Court selection affects forms, fees, rules, service, and deadlines.

2. Confirm Current Requirements

Check official court websites, local rules, clerk instructions, judge procedures, filing fees, and any forms or cover sheets required by that court.

3. Build a Deadline Calendar

Track filing dates, service dates, response deadlines, hearings, orders, and any deadline listed in a judge's standing order or scheduling order.

FAQ

Is this legal advice?

No. This website provides general procedural information and organization ideas. It does not tell you what claims to bring, what arguments to make, or what choices are best for your situation.

Can this replace a lawyer?

No. A lawyer can give legal advice based on facts, law, strategy, deadlines, and risk. This site is only a starting point for learning and organizing.

What should I verify?

Verify all rules, deadlines, filing requirements, fees, service rules, standing orders, and court instructions directly with the court or with a qualified legal professional.
